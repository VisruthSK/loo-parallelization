When browsing the codebase to find places where [parallelization](parallelize.md) could be useful, we found two potential speedups--in these places, we may benefit from vectorizing functions instead of parallelizing a loop. The more important of the two is in `pseudobma_weights()`, when performing a Bayesian bootstrap. [There](https://github.com/stan-dev/loo/blob/master/R/loo_model_weights.R#L334), we currently loop over Bayesian bootstrap iterations where it seems quite plausible to swap to matrix operations. This could potentially be a large speedup--we should first write tests for correctness, then some benchmarking wouldn't hurt in the PR. Similarly, though less interesting, is [this gradient calculation](https://github.com/stan-dev/loo/blob/master/R/loo_model_weights.R#L278) in `stacking_weights()`, just above the previous function. This is less interesting because we are looping over `K`, the number of models being compared, which is usually small. However, the loop there seems amenable to being reconstructed as matrix operations as well, so we may as well take the (potential) free performance. These seem like low hanging fruit and well worth the time to implement.
